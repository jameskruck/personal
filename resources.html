Resource Collection

This collection features a variety of sources that have shaped my approach to different aspects of education. You'll find resources in various formats, including research articles, books, blogs, and podcasts, organized by topic area.

Each section contains materials I've found valuable in my own practice and that I frequently recommend to colleagues. They represent a mix of foundational works and emerging perspectives that I believe contribute meaningfully to the field.

I continually update this collection as I discover new resources. If you think there's an important source missing or have recommendations to share, please let me know.

AI in Education

Books

  • Teaching with AI by Ethan Mollick & Lilach Mollick (2023)

    A practical guide for educators on integrating AI tools in the classroom, with strategies for creating AI-enhanced assignments and promoting AI literacy.

  • AI and Education: The Reality and the Potential by Wayne Holmes & Ilkka Tuomi (2022)

    An analysis of AI's current impact and future possibilities in education, with critical perspectives on implementation challenges.

Research Articles

  • Stöhr, C., Demazière, C., & Adawi, T. (2023). "ChatGPT and beyond: The generative AI revolution and higher education."

    A comprehensive review of the impacts of generative AI on higher education practices, examining both opportunities and challenges.

  • Roll, I., & Wylie, R. (2023). "Evolution or Revolution? Considering the future of educational technology research in the age of AI."

    An examination of how AI is reshaping educational technology research methodologies and implications for learning design.

Websites & Blogs

  • AI + Education Newsletter by Ethan Mollick

    Regular updates on AI applications in education, with practical teaching ideas and analysis of emerging tools and research.

  • Artificial Intelligence in Education (AIED) Society

    Professional organization providing resources, conference proceedings, and research briefs on AI in education.

EDI & Accessibility

Books

  • Culturally Responsive Teaching and The Brain by Zaretta Hammond (2014)

    Connects neuroscience research with culturally responsive teaching practices, providing a framework for creating more equitable learning environments.

  • Universal Design for Learning: Theory and Practice by Anne Meyer, David H. Rose, & David Gordon (2014)

    A comprehensive guide to UDL principles and implementation strategies for creating flexible, accessible learning experiences.

Research Articles

  • Crenshaw, K., Gotanda, N., Peller, G., & Thomas, K. (1995). "Critical race theory: The key writings that formed the movement."

    Foundational work on critical race theory and its applications to understanding systemic inequities in education.

  • Tobin, T. J., & Behling, K. T. (2018). "Reach everyone, teach everyone: Universal design for learning in higher education."

    A practical guide for implementing UDL principles in higher education contexts, with case studies and implementation strategies.

Online Resources

  • CAST UDL Guidelines

    Comprehensive framework for implementing Universal Design for Learning, with practical examples and resources.

  • Web Accessibility Initiative (WAI)

    Resources and guidelines for creating accessible digital content, including WCAG standards and implementation techniques.

Graduate School

Books

  • The Professor Is In: The Essential Guide to Turning Your Ph.D. Into a Job by Karen Kelsky (2015)

    Practical advice for navigating the academic job market, with strategies for CV development, interviews, and career planning.

  • Writing Your Journal Article in Twelve Weeks by Wendy Laura Belcher (2019)

    A structured approach to academic writing and publishing, with week-by-week guidance for manuscript development.

Articles & Guides

  • Golde, C. M. (2000). "Should I stay or should I go? Student descriptions of the doctoral attrition process."

    Analysis of factors influencing doctoral student persistence and strategies for overcoming common challenges.

  • Lovitts, B. E. (2008). "The transition to independent research: Who makes it, who doesn't, and why."

    Examination of the challenges in transitioning from coursework to independent research, with recommendations for support structures.

Podcasts & Blogs

  • The Scholarly Communication Lab Podcast

    Discussions on academic publishing, research workflows, and scholarly communication trends for graduate students and early-career researchers.

  • PhD Talk Blog by Eva Lantsoght

    Practical advice on research productivity, work-life balance, and academic career development for doctoral students.

Human Behaviour

Books

  • Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman (2011)

    Exploration of the two systems that drive human thinking: the fast, intuitive system and the slow, deliberative system, with implications for decision-making and learning.

  • Nudge: Improving Decisions About Health, Wealth, and Happiness by Richard H. Thaler & Cass R. Sunstein (2008)

    Overview of how choice architecture and environmental factors influence behavior, with applications for designing learning environments.

Research Papers

  • Deci, E. L., & Ryan, R. M. (2000). "Self-determination theory and the facilitation of intrinsic motivation, social development, and well-being."

    Foundational paper on self-determination theory and its applications to motivation and engagement in learning contexts.

  • Dweck, C. S. (2008). "Mindsets and math/science achievement."

    Examination of how growth mindset interventions impact student persistence and achievement in challenging subjects.

Podcasts & Videos

  • Hidden Brain Podcast by Shankar Vedantam

    Exploration of unconscious patterns that drive human behavior, with episodes particularly relevant to learning and education.

  • The Science of Well-Being (Yale Course) by Laurie Santos

    Evidence-based approaches to happiness and well-being, with practical applications for educational contexts and student support.

Instructional Design

Books

  • Design for How People Learn by Julie Dirksen (2015)

    A practical guide to creating effective learning experiences based on cognitive science principles, with a focus on learner engagement and memory.

  • Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning by Peter C. Brown, Henry L. Roediger III, & Mark A. McDaniel (2014)

    Research-based strategies for effective learning, with implications for course design and teaching practices.

Frameworks & Models

  • Wiggins, G., & McTighe, J. (2005). "Understanding by Design."

    Framework for curriculum design that emphasizes backward planning from learning outcomes, with practical templates and examples.

  • Merrill, M. D. (2002). "First principles of instruction."

    Five core principles for effective instructional design, applicable across various learning contexts and modalities.

Online Resources

  • The Learning Scientists

    Evidence-based learning strategies and tools for educators, with downloadable resources and implementation guides.

  • Connie Malamed's E-Learning Coach

    Practical guidance on instructional design for digital learning environments, with focus on visual design and cognitive approaches.

Teaching & Learning

Books

  • Small Teaching: Everyday Lessons from the Science of Learning by James M. Lang (2016)

    Practical, research-based teaching techniques that can be implemented without major course redesign, with focus on accessibility and impact.

  • How Learning Works: Seven Research-Based Principles for Smart Teaching by Susan A. Ambrose et al. (2010)

    Comprehensive overview of learning principles with practical teaching strategies, based on cognitive and educational psychology.

Research Articles

  • Freeman, S., et al. (2014). "Active learning increases student performance in science, engineering, and mathematics."

    Meta-analysis demonstrating the effectiveness of active learning approaches across STEM disciplines, with significant implications for teaching practice.

  • Hake, R. R. (1998). "Interactive-engagement versus traditional methods: A six-thousand-student survey of mechanics test data for introductory physics courses."

    Foundational study on the impact of interactive teaching methods on student learning outcomes in physics education.

Organizations & Resources

  • The Chronicle of Higher Education Teaching Newsletter

    Weekly updates on teaching innovations, research, and trends in higher education pedagogy.

  • POD Network (Professional and Organizational Development Network in Higher Education)

    Resources, conferences, and publications focused on faculty development and teaching improvement in higher education.

  • Faculty Focus

    Articles and resources on teaching strategies, online instruction, assessment, and classroom management for higher education.
