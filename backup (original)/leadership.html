Leadership Approach

As a leader, I create collaborative, inclusive environments where team members are equipped to take on challenges and committed to shared team success. My leadership is built on the belief that our best results arise from a team that draws on diverse perspectives, supports one another's growth, and aligns efforts with a clear strategic vision.

Collaboration

I prioritize peer review and open dialogue to encourage team members to learn from one another and share their unique viewpoints. This approach strengthens our outcomes and creates an environment where every voice is valued.

Empowerment

I'm committed to equipping my team with clear guidance, essential resources, and regular feedback. Whether we're developing a large-scale online course or navigating a high-stakes project, I ensure team members have the tools and support to confidently own their responsibilities.

Accountability

A strong team is built on trust and accountability. I develop a culture where team members feel responsible not just for their individual contributions but for our collective team goals. This shared sense of purpose drives us to meet deadlines, exceed expectations, and continuously grow together.

Leadership Philosophy

Foster Growth Through Community Building

For me, educational leadership grew from a recognition that the best teaching emerges from collaborative learning environments where instructors, developers, and learners contribute to shared growth. As a teaching assistant with minimal formal training, I initially approached teaching through trial and error. However, a pivotal classroom observation from my department chair pushed me towards evidence-based learning research, thereby sparking a career-long commitment to intentional, research-driven educational development.

Create Spaces for Authentic Dialogue and Growth

Great leaders create environments of mutual trust and continuous learning. When I transitioned to the private sector and inherited a team without formal learning and development backgrounds, I saw an opportunity to build a learning community. We began by sharing resources and best practices, but as our team grew to include members with diverse educational backgrounds, we evolved into a peer learning network, where diverse perspectives and experiences shaped our collective practice. To further my own development, I regularly put myself in the developer role, and invite feedback from team members on my approach.

Foster Trust Through Meaningful Connection

In one-on-one meetings, my team members and I prioritize establishing trust through open discussions about goals and challenges. The resultant relationship enables coaching opportunities that flow in both directions. Whether we're exploring universal design principles, sharing accessibility tips, or discussing emerging technologies like generative AI, every team member's voice influences our direction. Through dedicated communication channels for sharing learning articles and teaching innovations, we maintain an ongoing dialogue about educational excellence.

Model Mentorship and Lifelong Learning

My mentors have taught me invaluable lessons about inviting students into the learning process and acknowledging its complexity. Perhaps most importantly, they showed me that "I don't know, but I'll find out" is a response that models the continuous learning mindset we hope to instill in others. These experiences have shaped how I now mentor others, emphasizing humility and enthusiasm in the teaching process.

Balance Aspiration with Practicality

The best educational leaders help others navigate the challenging balance between aspiration and practicality. I've learned to guide team members in identifying their most critical project goals while emphasizing that improvement is an iterative process. This approach helps prevent frustration and burnout while maintaining momentum toward excellence. When we create supportive environments where people feel safe to experiment and grow incrementally, we foster sustainable development practices that benefit both educators and learners.

Challenge Complacency and Drive Continuous Growth

Looking ahead, I stay attentive to the need for ongoing improvement in teaching and development practices. By maintaining a dual focus on research-informed methods and supportive relationships, while constantly seeking new ways to improve, we can create lasting positive change in how we approach teaching and learning. Our role as educational leaders must include helping others maintain this balance of evidence-based practice and continuous growth.

Case Study: Team Structure and Career Development

Challenge

Several years ago, the team I was leading faced two significant challenges affecting our ability to produce:

  1. Bottlenecks in decision-making and project coordination, slowing down course development timelines and preventing long-term planning.
  2. A lack of clear career advancement opportunities for team members, resulting in stagnant roles, underutilized skills, and low morale.

The team managed a portfolio of over 250 active and inactive learning products, and the absence of defined roles and progression pathways was limiting scalability and team satisfaction.

Action

To address these challenges, I led a comprehensive department restructuring initiative, focusing on clear roles, distributed leadership, and career growth opportunities:

1. Creation of Team Lead Roles

  • Established Team Leads to handle project coordination and small-scale content decisions
  • Empowered Team Leads to manage their own project boards and collaborate with SMEs

2. Defined Career Progression Pathways

  • Introduced Junior and Intermediate ID roles for troubleshooting and updates
  • Elevated Senior IDs to focus on content creation and mentoring
  • Formalized the Editor role for consistent content quality

3. Enhanced Communication Processes

  • Implemented regular stand-ups and sprint planning
  • Clarified decision-making hierarchies

4. Focused on Talent Development

  • Introduced project-specific performance reviews
  • Established structured mentorship opportunities

Results

The restructuring initiative achieved measurable improvements across the department:

Improved Scalability

The creation of teams allowed for a staggered course kickoff, smoother workflows, and better resource allocation.

Enhanced Efficiency

Bottlenecks were reduced as Team Leads took ownership of coordination tasks, allowing the Curriculum Manager to focus on strategic priorities.

Career Growth Realized

Five team members advanced into senior or leadership roles within the organization, while two leveraged their experience for external opportunities.

Higher Team Morale

Clear career pathways and defined responsibilities boosted team satisfaction and long-term retention.
